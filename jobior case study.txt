  <section class="section process" id="process">
<div class="container">
<div class="section__label">Design Process</div>
<h2 class="section__title">From research to refined UI.</h2>
<p class="section__desc">Each phase was intentional — building understanding before building interfaces.</p>

<div class="process__steps">

<div class="process__step">
<div class="process__step-num">01</div>
<div class="process__step-body">
<h3>Research & Competitor Analysis</h3>
<p>
Audited LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor through the lens of a junior designer. The findings were consistent: dashboards were cluttered with irrelevant content, platforms forced sign-up before showing any value, and job cards communicated poorly — burying the most important details.
</p>
<div class="insight-chips">
<span class="chip chip--red">Cluttered dashboards</span>
<span class="chip chip--red">Forced premature sign-up</span>
<span class="chip chip--red">Low-signal job cards</span>
<span class="chip chip--green">Opportunity: junior-first UX</span>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="process__step">
<div class="process__step-num">02</div>
<div class="process__step-body">
<h3>Moodboard & Visual Direction</h3>
<p>
I curated moodboards to define the visual tone before any UI work began. Green emerged as the primary color — representing freshness, growth, and the energy of someone at the start of their career. Poppins was chosen for its geometric clarity and approachable weight — it reads as modern without feeling corporate.
</p>
<div class="palette">
<div class="swatch" style="background:#1db954;" title="Primary Green"></div>
<div class="swatch" style="background:#145c32;" title="Deep Green"></div>
<div class="swatch" style="background:#111111;" title="Near Black"></div>
<div class="swatch" style="background:#f5f5f5;" title="Off White"></div>
<div class="swatch" style="background:#f2c94c;" title="Accent Yellow"></div>
</div>
<p class="palette__note">Black illustrations paired with green created high contrast — bold, legible, and visually distinct.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="process__step">
<div class="process__step-num">03</div>
<div class="process__step-body">
<h3>Wireframing & Information Architecture</h3>
<p>
Separate flows were mapped for job seekers and recruiters — their goals diverge significantly, so the architecture reflects that. A key structural decision: the search bar appears on the home screen <em>before</em> authentication. This lets job seekers immediately evaluate whether the platform has relevant opportunities, reducing drop-off before sign-up.
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="process__step">
<div class="process__step-num">04</div>
<div class="process__step-body">
<h3>UI Design & Prototyping</h3>
<p>
Wireframes were translated into high-fidelity screens using the established design system. Interactive prototypes in Figma allowed for realistic flow testing across both user types. Screens were iterated based on feedback, with particular attention to card hierarchy, CTA placement, and mobile thumb-zone ergonomics.
</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
